# VIDEO B1 — Insurance Explanation: Version A (Unbundled)

**Duration:** 28–30 seconds | **Aspect:** 16:9 landscape | **Resolution:** 1280×720 | **Format:** MP4 (H.264)

---

## Purpose

This is a **core research instrument.** Participants pay 1 token to watch it. It explains how insurance works when seeds and insurance are offered as **separate products** (Version A of the experiment).

The video must communicate the exact payoff structure: seeds cost 10, insurance costs 2 extra (12 total), good rain pays 30 from seeds and 0 from insurance, bad rain pays 0 from seeds but 10 from insurance.

**The video must NOT advocate for or against buying insurance.** Neutral, factual presentation only.

---

## Critical Numbers (MUST appear on screen)

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Seed cost | 10 tokens |
| Insurance cost | 2 tokens |
| Total (seeds + insurance) | 12 tokens |
| Good rain — seed payout | 30 tokens |
| Good rain — insurance payout | 0 tokens |
| Bad rain — seed payout | 0 tokens |
| Bad rain — insurance payout | 10 tokens |

---

## Narration Script (~60 words, very tight — pace ~120 wpm)

> "Seeds cost 10 tokens. Insurance costs 2 tokens more — 12 tokens total.
>
> If there is good rain: seeds earn you 30 tokens. Insurance pays nothing.
>
> If there is bad rain: seeds earn you nothing — your crop fails. But insurance pays you back 10 tokens, covering the cost of your seeds.
>
> Either way, you are not left with nothing."

## Visual Requirements

- **10 + 2 = 12 equation** must be visually unambiguous. Show actual coin counts, not just numbers.
- **Good rain vs. bad rain** should use the same visual language as Videos A4/A5 for consistency.
- **Insurance payout moment** (10 coins from shield) is the climactic visual beat — give it emphasis but not drama.
- **Insurance shield "0"** in good rain must clearly show insurance exists but did nothing — it's grayed out, not absent.
- **Final comparison** must be balanced — neither outcome should look "better" or "worse." Both columns get equal visual treatment.
- All numbers must be readable at arm's length on a 10-inch tablet.

---

## Audio

- Narration only, no music.
- Pace: steady 120 wpm. No rushed sections — every number gets a beat of silence after it.
- Tone: neutral, informational. Warm but not persuasive. The narrator is explaining facts, not selling.
- The final line ("not left with nothing") can have a slightly warmer, gentler tone.

---

## Research Integrity Note

This video is identical in structure to Video B2 (bundled version). The ONLY difference is how the products are framed: here, seeds (10) and insurance (2) are presented as **separate purchases**. In B2, they are presented as a **single bundle (12)**. This difference is the core experimental manipulation. Do not change the payoff numbers or structure without consulting the research team.
